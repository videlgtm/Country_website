Introduction

Albania is a beautiful country in Southeastern Europe with many interesting landmarks and attractions. One of the most famous sites is the ancient city of Butrint, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Butrint was once a busy Greek and Roman city, and today, visitors can see the remains of temples, theaters, and old city walls. The ruins are surrounded by nature, making it a great place to explore both history and the outdoors.

Landmarks

  • Gjirokastër "City of Stone.": This town is famous for its old stone houses and a giant castle on a hill. The Gjirokastër Castle has been around since the 12th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Inside the castle, you can find a museum with displays about Albania’s history, and there's even a weapons collection from the country’s past.

Attractions

Albania is full of attractions for a traveler, which amply blends its rich history with stunning landscapes and beautiful coastlines. Both Gjirokastër and Berat are UNESCO World Heritage sites, famous for their well-preserved Ottoman architecture and ancient castles that give insight into the country's past. The different landscapes of Albania will charm the nature lovers, from the peaks of the Albanian Alps to the virgin beaches of Ksamil and Dhermi along the Ionian and Adriatic Seas. The ancient city of Butrint, listed as a UNESCO archaeological site, offers impressive ruins that span Greek, Roman, Byzantine, and Venetian periods. Equally, the views over the coastline from the Llogara Pass are breathtaking, and the capital, Tirana, has a lively arts scene and several animated cafes. From historical site exploration to hiking in nature and relaxing at the sea, Albania provides a mix of culture, adventure, and beauty.
