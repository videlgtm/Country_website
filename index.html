Introduction

Albania is a small, seemingly beautiful country in Southeastern Europe, at the Balkan Peninsula; it borders Montenegro to the northwest, Kosovo to the north, North Macedonia to the east, and Greece to the south. The country has a coastline along the Adriatic and Ionian Seas on the west and south. Albania boasts very beautiful landscapes from its mountains to beaches and forests, and a rich cultural heritage with unique traditions.

Aspects of Albania

  • Culture & Traditions: Albania has a great history blended with Greek, Roman, Ottoman, and Byzantine influences. Tourists can be treated to traditional Albanian folk music, dance, and festivals such as the "Dita e Verës" or Spring Day, which is one celebration to welcome spring.
  • Cuisine: Albanian food was the result of a mix of Mediterranean cuisines along with influences from neighbors. The popular dishes comprise byrek, which is savory pastry; fërgesë is mainly vegetables and cheese, while tavë kosi is lamb with yogurt. The country has also produced very good wines and olive oil.
  • Landmarks & Attractions: Albania has also several UNESCO World Heritage sites, such as the ancient city of Butrint and the medieval town of Gjirokastër. One can also visit the wonderful beaches on the Albanian Riviera or hike in the Albanian Alps.
